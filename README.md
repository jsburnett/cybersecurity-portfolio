
## About Me
I'm a Marine veteran turned IT leader based in Alabama, currently serving as CTO at a local rural electric cooperative. When I'm not knee-deep in infrastructure, I'm probably watching one of my three kids compete in something, gaming, or listening to a new Audible book. Charlie the dog keeps me humble.

I grew up doing HVAC and electrical work before finding my way into IT — no CS degree to lean on, just a lot of broken things that needed fixing and a stubborn refusal to leave them that way. That path eventually led me to managing enterprise infrastructure for a critical utility: multi-domain Active Directory, VMware, Fortinet security stack, SCADA/OT networks, and the kind of systems where downtime isn't just an inconvenience. I've handled everything from DC promotions at 2am to incident response on production environments, and I've learned that the best documentation is the kind you write before something breaks.

In recent years my focus has shifted heavily toward cybersecurity — continuously expanding my knowledge and pushing deeper into the discipline. I hold several GIAC certifications including GSEC, GCIH, and GCIA, and I'm currently finishing my Bachelor's in Applied Cybersecurity at SANS Technology Institute. I hope this portfolio serves as a journal of sorts for that journey — a place to track what I'm learning, document tools I build, and collect resources worth keeping.
